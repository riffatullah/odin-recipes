Dumpukht

Description

Dum pukht (Persian: دُم‌ پخت), larhmeen, or slow oven cooking is a cooking technique associated with the Mughal Empire in which meat and vegetables are cooked over a low flame, generally in dough-sealed containers[1] with few spices.[citation needed] Traditions assign its origin in pre-partition India to the reign of Nawab of Awadh Asaf-ud-Daulah (1748–97).[2] The technique is now commonly used in other cuisines such as South Asian, Central Asian, and West Asian

Ingredients

  • Mutton 1 Kg
  • Goat Fat 1 Kg
  • Potato 5 small size
  • Tomato 5 small size
  • Salt as required
  • Green Chilli 4 pieces

Steps for Cooking Dumpukht

  1. Layer the goat fat at the bottom of a deep pot and make a layer of mutton on top of it
  2. Set the vegetables over it and sprinkle salt over it
  3. Now cook it for several hours on low heat while cover the pot tightly to stop evaporation from it
